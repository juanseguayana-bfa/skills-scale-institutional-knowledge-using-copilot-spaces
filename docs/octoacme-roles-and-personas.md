# OctoAcme Roles and Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Core Delivery Roles

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Interaction Points
- Partner with Product Managers to clarify acceptance criteria and scope trade-offs
- Partner with Project Managers on delivery sequencing and risk escalation
- Partner with QA Leads on test strategy and release-readiness criteria

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Interaction Points
- Partner with Project Managers to align roadmap priorities with delivery constraints
- Partner with Developers and Technical Leads on feasibility and technical trade-offs
- Partner with UX Designers to validate user outcomes before implementation

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Interaction Points
- Partner with Product Managers on scope, priorities, and milestone decisions
- Partner with Technical Leads and Developers on dependency and execution planning
- Partner with Release Managers and Support/Operations to coordinate launch readiness

---

## Supporting Roles

## Technical Leads

### Role Summary
Technical Leads guide technical direction, architecture decisions, and engineering execution quality across the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define and document architecture choices and technical constraints
- Lead estimation input for complex or high-risk work
- Surface technical risks early and propose mitigation options
- Coach developers on implementation quality and maintainability

### Goals
- Keep technical decisions aligned with product outcomes and delivery timelines
- Reduce rework by clarifying architecture and integration choices early

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews with developers
- Feasibility and trade-off discussions with PM and PdM
- Risk updates in planning and execution syncs

### Interaction Points
- Align with Product Managers on solution feasibility and scope options
- Align with Project Managers on milestone impacts and dependency sequencing
- Align with QA Leads on quality gates and non-functional requirements

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers define user flows, interaction patterns, and usability standards to ensure delivered solutions meet user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Create user flows, wireframes, and interaction guidance
- Validate usability assumptions through lightweight testing or feedback loops
- Support acceptance criteria with user-centered requirements
- Collaborate on design implementation quality during execution

### Goals
- Improve usability and reduce friction in critical user journeys
- Ensure design intent remains clear from planning through release

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with PdM and developers
- Usability findings shared in planning and demo sessions
- Documentation of design decisions for delivery teams

### Interaction Points
- Align with Product Managers on user outcomes and feature priorities
- Align with Developers and Technical Leads on implementation feasibility
- Align with QA Leads on usability acceptance checks

---

## QA Leads

### Role Summary
QA Leads own the test strategy, quality gates, and release-readiness checks for project deliverables.

### Responsibilities
- Define risk-based test approach for each milestone
- Ensure acceptance criteria are testable and measurable
- Coordinate test execution coverage (unit, integration, and smoke tests)
- Recommend go/no-go quality status for releases

### Goals
- Reduce escaped defects and improve release confidence
- Build repeatable quality practices across teams

### Typical Communication
- Test strategy and quality updates in planning and weekly syncs
- Defect triage with developers and PM
- Release-readiness recommendations before deployment

### Interaction Points
- Align with Project Managers on quality risks and release timing
- Align with Developers and Technical Leads on defect resolution priorities
- Align with Release Managers on go/no-go criteria

---

## Release Managers

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate the operational execution of deployments, including readiness checks, rollout communication, and rollback preparedness.

### Responsibilities
- Prepare and track release runbooks and readiness checklists
- Coordinate deployment windows and cross-team dependencies
- Confirm rollback plans and ownership before production rollout
- Communicate release status and outcomes to stakeholders

### Goals
- Deliver predictable, low-risk releases with clear accountability
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and confusion

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness meetings with PM, QA, and engineering
- Deployment status updates to stakeholders
- Post-release follow-up notes and actions

### Interaction Points
- Align with Project Managers on release timing and external commitments
- Align with QA Leads on release quality gates and sign-off status
- Align with Support/Operations on production readiness and monitoring

---

## Support/Operations Representatives

### Role Summary
Support/Operations Representatives provide production context, operational constraints, and incident learnings to improve delivery decisions and stability.

### Responsibilities
- Share customer-impact insights and recurring incident patterns
- Validate monitoring, alerting, and support readiness before release
- Participate in incident triage and post-incident action tracking
- Feed operational learnings back into planning and prioritization

### Goals
- Improve production stability and incident response readiness
- Ensure delivery plans account for operational realities

### Typical Communication
- Weekly risk and operations update inputs
- Release readiness confirmations with PM and Release Manager
- Incident and support trend summaries to PdM and engineering

### Interaction Points
- Align with Project Managers on escalation workflows and incident ownership
- Align with Product Managers on prioritizing production-impacting improvements
- Align with Release Managers and QA Leads on operational readiness checks
